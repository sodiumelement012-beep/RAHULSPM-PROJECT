Bihar Tourism offers a captivating journey through ancient history, spirituality, and vibrant culture. From the spiritual enlightenment at Bodh Gaya to the ancient academic ruins of Nalanda, the state beautifully blends heritage, ecology, and monumental landmarks like Patna's Golghar and the holy banks of the Ganges.

Buddhist Circuit: Walk in the footsteps of Lord Buddha. Visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Mahabodhi Temple in Bodh Gaya and the ancient monastic ruins at Nalanda University.Jain & Sikh Heritage: Pay your respects at Vaishali, the birthplace of Lord Mahavir, or visit Takht Sri Patna Sahib, one of the holiest shrines in Sikhism.Ramayana Circuit: Trace the legendary footsteps of Lord Rama and Sita with visits to Sitamarhi, the revered birthplace of Goddess Sita.

Nalanda University is a prestigious international research university located in Rajgir, Bihar, India. Established in 2010 as a successor to the ancient seat of learning, it focuses on postgraduate and doctoral studies in areas like ecology, sustainable development, Buddhist studies, and Asian history.

Status: Designated as an "Institute of National Importance" by the Government of India, operating under the Ministry of External Affairs.Academics: Offers exclusively Master's and PhD programs. Focus areas include School of Buddhist Studies, Philosophy & Comparative Religions; Ecology & Environment Studies; and Historical Studies.The Campus: Sprawls over a massive, eco-friendly, carbon-neutral "net-zero" campus designed to fuse ancient wisdom with modern, world-class amenities.Ancient Legacy: The original Nalanda Mahavihara was founded in the 5th century AD and is considered one of the world's oldest residential universities. It historically hosted over 10,000 students from across Asia before being destroyed in the 12th century

Nalanda University was established to emulate the ancient university of Nalanda (Nalanda mahavihara), which functioned between the 5th and 13th centuries. The idea to resurrect Nalanda University was endorsed in 2007 at the 2nd East Asia Summit by the sixteen member countries.[18] In 2009, during the 4th East Asia summit, ASEAN member states including Australia, China, Korea, Singapore and Japan promised further support.[26] The Government of Bihar handed over land acquired from local people, to the university for its new campus.[27][28][29][30][31] Chief Minister of Bihar Nitish Kumar also met External Affairs Minister SM Krishna to receive reassurance that the Central Government would allocate sufficient funds to the project.[32]

Rajgir

The Rajgir Glass Bridge is India's first glass skywalk, located within the Rajgir Nature Safari in Bihar. Suspended 200 feet above a scenic gorge between the Vaibhagiri and Songiri hills, this 85-foot-long transparent bridge offers thrilling panoramic views.
